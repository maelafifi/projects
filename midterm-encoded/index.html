SFFD Data Visualizations

Our team visualizes San Francisco Fire Department Calls in December 2016 using Tableau and the D3.js library.

Learn More

Data & Processing

Data for the San Francisco Fire department was published by the City and County of San Francisco and found on SF OpenData. Our team used a spreadsheet software called Trifacta Wrangler to create a subset of the data we would use on our visualizations. Our subset includes call date, dispatch time, call type, call priority, and neighborhood district, resulting in a dataset that is 17,533 rows by 20 columns. Further details on each column can be found here. In order to make the data as consistent as possible, our prototypes only consider unique call numbers due to the fact that each call can result in the dispatching of multiple units. Missing and mismatched data is removed from our dataset. We provide a list of columns with their data type and description. In order to reproduce our subset, we also provide the option to download our Trifacta script below.
