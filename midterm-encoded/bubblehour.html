What's the busiest hour for the SF Fire Department?

On this page, you'll find a bubble plot that explains the busiest times of the day for the San Fracnisco Fire Department, specifically for the month of December, 2016. The visual takes the hour of the day, as well as the count of distinct calls for that particular hour, throughout the month.

San Francisco Fire Department: Busy Hours

Source: SF Open Data

https://data.sfgov.org/Public-Safety/Fire-Department-Calls-for-Service-December-2016/97i8-upbq?category=Public-Safety&view_name=Fire-Department-Calls-for-Service-December-2016

Full-Sreen Visualization

This bubbe chart had plenty of information embedded within it. As you can see, there are particular times of the day where there are obviously more incidents happening in San Francisco, while there are also particular times where there are very few incidents happening. Just from looking at the graph, you see a trend: during the hours when most people are asleep, there are fewer incidents, and during the hours when most people are awake, there are far more incidents. Although this data can be somewhat intuitive, it is interesting to see the consistency of calls during particular hours of the day.

I decided to implement this type of graph because I've had experience with this type of graph. I've also implemented it for a different data sets, and I was slightly unimpressed by the end result, so I wanted to do better on this type of graph.

The data for this bubbleplot chart was derived from the subset of data that Elia derived. Simply speaking, I utilized the distinct count(a function in tableau), and matched it up against the hours of the day(which was derived by Elia when creating the subset of Data for our group), and created a smaller data set based on that. As you might imagine, the resulting data set exported contained twenty-four rows(number of hours in a week), and two columns(one for hour of the day, and the other for the distinct call count).
