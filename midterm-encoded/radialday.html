What's the busiest day for the SF Fire Department?

On this page, you'll find a stacked radial bar chart that sums up all incidents for the San Francisco Fire Department in the month of December, 2016. The sum of all incidents is then divided up by the day that each incident occurred on, and the visual gives a clear view of what days are busiest and what days are slowest.

Full-Sreen Visualization

This radial bar chart has a lot of information inside of it. From the very first look, you can see explicit days where there are higher caller counts, and it's all towards the end of the week: Thursday, Friday, and Saturday. While it is not known for sure, I have assumed that it's because there are a lot more people out and doing things on those days, especially at later times. In contrast to that, the days of the week where there are the fewest amount of calls are generally days where people spend most of their day working and/or resting from the work they had just accomplished. Sunday, the only "slow" day that is not a work day is likely slow because many people utilize this day of the week as a relaxation day, so fewer incidents might happen when there is less commotion.

I decided to implement this radial chart because the days of the weeks are repeating and in the same order. Moreover, I've only seen a few of these types of graphs implemented, so I thought it would be nice to implement something that few people have had an exeperience with. I hope you enjoyed it as much as I enjoyed it!

The data for this radial bar chart was derived from the subset of data that Elia derived. Simply speaking, I utilized the distinct count(a function in tableau), and matched it up against the days of the week(which were in number format[1-7]), and created a smaller data set based on that. As you might imagine, the resulting data set exported contained seven rows(number of days in a week), and two columns(one for day of the week, and the other for the distinct count).
