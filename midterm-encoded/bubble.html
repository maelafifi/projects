SFFD Call Incidents in December 2016

This bubble chart explores the frequency of incidents per call type for the SFFD in December 2016. The larger the bubble, the more frequent the incident. Contrary to what one might expect, the majority of calls that took place at that time were of a medical nature. Even if we were to consider all other incident types and sum their frequencies, their joint sum would still be less than the occurences of medical incidents! If we hover over each bubble, we're also able to see the exact count for the call type.

Another discovery, regardless of how counterintuitive it may be, is how little the SFFD actually assists the police in incidents. Naturally, we would assume that safety departments collaborate very often to protect their citzens or at least document joint operations. Additionally, our team has not been able to find clear descriptions of categories such as 'Alarms' and 'Other'. We only know that they took a substantial amount of time and resources to deal with due to the high number of occurences. Given the ambiguity of the descriptions for each incident type, further details are needed to better understand the visual narrative.
